# Groundwork: cold outreach templates

Target: B2B SaaS founders / CTOs at pre-seed to seed startups (2-30 people)
selling to businesses. The hook is the moment a security questionnaire or SOC 2
request lands, usually right as deals get bigger.

## Rules that make this work

- **Always personalize the first line with a real trigger** (a funding round, a
  launch, a first enterprise hire, a "we just landed X" post). If you cannot
  find a trigger, skip that lead. One specific line beats a whole templated
  email.
- Keep it short. One idea, one link, one soft ask.
- Send as a real person with a real signature. 10-20 per day by hand at first.
- Tuesday to Thursday, business hours, land best.
- Email: identify yourself and include an opt-out. Use a separate sending
  domain so a spam flag never touches groundwork-security.com.

Tokens: `{FirstName}`, `{Company}`, `{trigger}`, `{YourName}`.

---

## Email

### Subject lines (pick one, keep it lowercase and low-key)

- security questionnaire for {Company}?
- before {Company}'s next enterprise deal
- {Company} + SOC 2
- quick one for {FirstName}

### First email

> Hi {FirstName},
>
> Congrats on {trigger}. As {Company}'s deals get bigger, a security
> questionnaire or a SOC 2 request usually isn't far behind, and it tends to
> land right when you're busiest.
>
> I built Groundwork for exactly that moment. You answer a few questions about
> what {Company} builds and runs, and it gives you a prioritized, plain
> language security plan: what to do this week, this month, and this quarter.
> It's free.
>
> Worth a look before the next big customer asks? groundwork-security.com
>
> {YourName}
> Groundwork · groundwork-security.com
> Not interested? Reply "no thanks" and I won't follow up.

### Follow-up 1 (3-4 days later, same thread)

> Hi {FirstName}, quick nudge on this. The free plan takes about 5 minutes and
> flags the gaps most likely to stall an enterprise deal. Happy to walk through
> {Company}'s results if that's useful. groundwork-security.com

### Follow-up 2 (about a week later, breakup)

> Hi {FirstName}, I'll leave it here so I'm not cluttering your inbox. If a
> security questionnaire ever lands on your desk, Groundwork will get you ready
> fast: groundwork-security.com. Wishing {Company} the best.

## LinkedIn

### Connection request (300 char limit)

> Hi {FirstName}, I build Groundwork, a free tool that gives startups a tailored
> security plan before the first big customer sends a security questionnaire.
> Given what you're building at {Company}, thought it might be useful. Would
> love to connect.

### First message (after they accept)

> Thanks for connecting, {FirstName}. Not pitching, I genuinely think Groundwork
> could save {Company} a scramble later. It turns "what's your security
> posture?" into a 5-minute, prioritized plan, and it's free. If you ever get a
> security questionnaire or kick off a SOC 2 push, worth a look:
> groundwork-security.com. Happy to answer anything.

### Follow-up (a few days later)

> No worries if now isn't the time, I'll stop here. If security ever turns into
> a deal blocker, the free plan is at groundwork-security.com. Good luck with
> {Company}.

## Trigger ideas to personalize {trigger}

- "the seed round" / "the raise"
- "launching {Company}" / "the Product Hunt launch"
- "hiring your first enterprise AE" (means enterprise deals are coming)
- "landing {Customer}" (means questionnaires are coming)
- "the SOC 2 post" (they already said they're starting)
- a specific detail from their site: "building {product} for {audience}"

## Don't

- Don't attach files or stack multiple links.
- Don't fake familiarity or use "Dear Sir/Madam."
- Don't send without a real, specific first line.
- Don't blast the same copy to hundreds at once, especially early.
