# Groundwork: Edmonton, Alberta leads

Local is an advantage. Edmonton founders are far likelier to reply to someone
in their own city, and you can offer a coffee instead of a cold link. Use that.

## Best local databases (this is where the real list comes from)

Edmonton has unusually good, free startup directories. Browse these, filter for
small/early software companies, and grab the site + founder for each:

- **Start Alberta - startalberta.ca** - the most comprehensive Alberta startup
  and funding database (run by A100, powered by Dealroom). Filter by city
  (Edmonton) and stage. This is your primary source.
- **Startup TNT - startuptnt.com** - local angel network; their summit companies
  and portfolio are all early-stage Alberta startups actively raising.
- **Edmonton Unlimited - edmontonunlimited.com** - the city's startup hub;
  companies in their programs are early and reachable.
- **Amii - amii.ca** - the AI institute; good for AI startups specifically.
- **Wellfound (Edmonton) and StartupBlink (Edmonton)** - StartupBlink lists 300+
  Edmonton startups; Wellfound shows who is hiring (a sign they are growing).

## Playing the local card

- **Meet in person.** Offer a 15-minute coffee. "I'm building a startup security
  tool here in Edmonton and would love your take" gets meetings a cold email never will.
- **Work the events:** Startup Week Edmonton, Upper Bound (the big Edmonton AI
  conference), Startup TNT summits, and Edmonton Unlimited / Amii meetups. These
  rooms are full of exactly these founders.
- **Lead with local in the email subject:** "fellow Edmonton founder + a security
  question" or "quick one from another Edmonton startup."
