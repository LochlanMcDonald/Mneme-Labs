SEC. 02

The work it takes off your hands

TALOS is not a demo platform. It is deployed against three categories of labor — the dull, the heavy, and the dangerous.

A — THE DULL ↗

Repetitive material handling

Tote moves, kitting, machine tending, palletizing. TALOS runs the same task ten thousand times with the same precision as the first — and never checks its phone.

B — THE HEAVY ↗

Lifting past human limits

A 25 kg sustained payload with full-body dynamic balance. TALOS lifts from the floor, carries over uneven ground, and stacks overhead — shift after shift, injury-free by design.

C — THE DANGEROUS ↗

Going where people shouldn't

Confined spaces, live equipment rooms, extreme heat and cold. Send the machine in first, keep the humans on the safe side of the door.

Why we build

TALOS is named for the bronze giant of Greek myth — the first machine humanity ever imagined, built to walk the perimeter and keep people safe. Twenty-eight centuries later, MNEME Labs is building it for real.

There are millions of jobs that injure the people who do them. Our position is simple: if a task is dull, heavy, or dangerous, it is a robot's job. We will keep shipping hardware until that sentence sounds obvious.
